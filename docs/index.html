Method, sources & caveats

  • Distances are straight-line from the HDB building centroid — an easy, consistent yardstick, but real walks are longer (roads, blocks, canals). MRT/LRT distance is to the station (the mean position of its exits).
  • The score (0–10) is a weighted sum of eight components, ordered by how much they shape daily life: rent value 2.0 > MRT/LRT 1.8 > hawker centre 1.5 > supermarket 1.4 > mall 1.2 > primary school 1.0 > infant care 0.7 > community club 0.4 (weights sum to 10; the ordering is editorial). Each amenity pays its full weight if the nearest one is within its benchmark distance, half within 1.5× the benchmark ("a stretch, but walkable"), 0 beyond. Benchmarks: supermarket & hawker 500 m, MRT & infant care 800 m, school / mall / CC 1 km. Having several of one type nearby doesn't add points — it shows in the block panel instead.
  • Rent value (the heaviest component): a block's rentals over the last 12 months are compared with the island-wide average for the same flat types (so a 3-room block isn't "cheap" just for being small): average ratio ≤ 0.95 → full 2.0 pts, ≤ 1.10 → 1.0, above → 0. Blocks with fewer than 3 rentals use their town's ratio (their own average is still displayed, marked accordingly); blocks in towns with no data score a neutral 1.0. Rents are HDB rental-approval declarations (data.gov.sg) — owner-declared, whole-flat rentals, indicative only. ~66% of blocks have their own transactions; the panel shows the average and the count.
  • "Best block" claims come with caveats: the ranking table keeps only blocks with a known dwelling-unit count (excludes carparks/commercial HDB buildings), straight-line distance flatters blocks near barriers (canals, expressways), and the weights are one editorial view of what matters — a family optimising for schools would weigh differently.
  • Benchmarks are editorial yardsticks (adjustable above for the charts), not official standards.
  • HDB blocks: HDB Existing Building footprints (data.gov.sg) — includes every HDB building, so some points are multi-storey carparks or commercial blocks.
  • Supermarkets: SFA licensed-supermarket register (data.gov.sg), filtered to NTUC FairPrice and Sheng Siong licences, geocoded by postal code via OneMap. Licence address ≈ store address. Other chains (Giant, Cold Storage, Prime…) are excluded by design.
  • Primary schools: MOE School Directory (data.gov.sg), PRIMARY and mixed-level (P1–S4) schools, geocoded by postal code. Note: home-to-school priority uses 1 km/2 km as defined by SLA for the address, which differs slightly from centroid distance.
  • Malls: editorial list of ~110 major malls (Wikipedia), geocoded via OneMap search — boutique/strip malls omitted.
  • MRT/LRT: LTA station-exit layer (data.gov.sg), including LRT; a station's position is the mean of its exits. When a block sits between two stations, the runner-up is called out in its panel.
  • Infant care: ECDA Listing of Centres — the 890 pre-schools whose infant places aren't marked "Not Applicable"; located via the ECDA Child Care Services layer / OneMap.
  • Hawker centres: NEA register (data.gov.sg). Community clubs: PA.
